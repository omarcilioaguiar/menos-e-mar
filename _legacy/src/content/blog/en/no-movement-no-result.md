Many people spend months planning the "perfect scenario." They wait to have the best camera to start a channel, the best keyboard to start coding, or the ideal Monday to start a diet.

The truth, as uncomfortable as it may be, is that **perfection is just a polite excuse for procrastination**.

In my most recent video, I talk about a pillar that governs my project *Menos e Mar*: the importance of the initial movement. Without action, the best plan in the world is just paper (or ones and zeros on a hard drive).

> "Done is better than perfect but undone."

## The Power of Reverse Inertia

When we are standing still, every small obstacle seems like an insurmountable mountain. But the moment you generate movement — even if it's a small, imperfect, and even slightly crooked step — the physics of life changes in your favor.

Movement brings clarity. You don't find out if the path is right by looking at the map from afar; you find out by walking and adjusting the route.

## Real Impact vs. Imaginary Planning

We live in a culture that idolizes planning but forgets about execution. Having *fewer* resources is often the best thing that can happen because it forces you to use creativity to move.

The "Menos" (Less) frees us from the weight of expectations, and the "Mar" (Sea, or Marcílio) reminds us that fluidity and consistency are what truly shape the rocks of our goals.

## Start where you are

If you are waiting for the green light to start that personal project, here it is: 🟢. Don't wait for total clarity; it only appears to those who are already on the road.
