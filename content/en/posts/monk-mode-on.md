Recently, I realized my attention was fragmented. Between notifications, infinite browser tabs, and the constant pressure to be "connected," genuine focus became a scarce resource. That's when I decided to adopt the so-called **Monk Mode**.

If you're not familiar with the term, Monk Mode is a period of intentional isolation and removal of distractions to work on something that really matters. For me, as a Software Engineer and minimalism enthusiast, this strategy was the perfect antidote to digital anxiety.

> "Focus isn't saying yes to what you want to do, but saying no to the thousand other good ideas out there."

## Why enter the cave?

We live in an attention economy. Everything around us is designed to take us out of the present moment. In the video below, I explain in detail how this change in posture helped me regain productivity and, more importantly, mental peace.

The main points I share are:

*   **Radical noise elimination**: How I cleaned my setup and disabled non-value-adding notifications.
*   **Deep Work**: The technique of immersing in complex tasks without interruptions.
*   **Anxiety Management**: How information overload generates a false sense of urgency.
